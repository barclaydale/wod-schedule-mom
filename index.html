What You'll Need Pair of Dumbbells
Intended Stimulus

Pacing: Smooth (because of the awkwardness of the dumbbells) but quick (to decrease time under tension and save your forearms). Ideally, you'll take two breaks each round, breaking with one rep left on the Deadlifts and again with one rep left on the Hang Power Cleans. 11 Deadlifts, break. 1 Deadlift, 8 Hang Power Cleans, break. 1 Hang Power Clean, 6 Push Jerks. This is an intentional strategy since the work to get the dumbbells into position after each break is the movement before it. Why do extra work if you don't have to?

Feel: Forearms...get ready. This is grippy. Pick a weight that would allow you to do the first two rounds of Hang Power Cleans and the Push Jerks unbroken. Probably 15#s, maybe 20#s if you feel confident.


Note: 1) Later on, we will do DT with a barbell at 60#. We have to build up the comfort and strength with the Hang Power Clean and the Push Jerk first! 2) Dumbbells are a little more awkward than a barbell. We're changing what would be a heavier barbell to lighter dumbbells which require greater stabaliization.

What You'll Need Red Band
Barbell
Intended Stimulus

Pacing: Grace is all about minimizing rest time. We're attempting to do a big set unbroken to start (5-8) then doubles or singles after that up to 30. Find something to keep you in a rhythm and do it for every rep. This could be counting to 5 starting when the bar touches the ground, two big breaths, or anything else you want. Do your best not to step away from the bar. The moment you step away, you don't want to come back. Stay in it until all 30 reps are completed. Shoot for 1 rep every 10s (6 per minute) for a time of 5:00. After your first big set, you should already have stored 10-30s as a buffer on the back end.

Feel: It hits you hard around rep 18-21. Despite being a weightlifting workout, Grace taxes your lungs more than anything. Mentally, the first 10-12 reps are easy. Getting through the next 10 is the hardest part. Once you hit rep 21-23, it's all downhill!


Note: Enter your time under the Light option of the Proficiency section as well.

Note: Also, while it says Grace is performed with Jerks, Push Press (and even Shoulder Press, though I do not recommend them) are acceptable movements. Grace is essentially 30 reps of getting the bar from the ground to the shoulder then from the shoulder to overhead, anyway you'd like.

What You'll Need Red Band
Med Ball
Intended Stimulus

Pacing: As your shoulders fatigue and the rest you get in your Wall Ball minute decreases, delay when you start your Wall Walk by upwards of 15s (unless your shoulders are feeling good). Wall Balls should remain unbroken! Reminder: for alternating EMOMs you do one movement per minute so the first four minutes will be 1 Wall Walk, then 4 Wall Balls the next minute, then 1 Wall Walk the next minute, then 5 Wall Balls the next minute... There's rest built in to the entire workout.

Feel: This is designed to blow up your shoulders. Your breathing will increase during the Wall Balls, but you should struggle more with the shoulder stamina as you progress. Be smart by using your legs as much as possible during the Wall Balls to rest your shoulders.


Note: Wall Walks are a new movement that are extremely demanding on your shoulders. A prescribed Wall Walk finishes in a handstand. You will only go as far as is comfortable. Some example distances to use are: until your hands touch the blue yoga mat, one gray square away from the wall, any other marked off distance. If your shoulders are feeling great and you're confident with your midline strength, go for the full Wall Walk! At the top of the handstand, there should be less than 10" of space between your hands and the wall. Don't worry about measuring it out if you plan on doing the full Wall Walk. Anywhere around a foot away is awesome. Whatever inverting you decide on is more than you've done in years so it's going to be a successful day no matter what you choose!
